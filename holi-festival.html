All the colors have they meaning.

During Holi, each color will have a special meaning. Red will symbolize love and fertility,, green will stand for new beginnings and nature, and blue will be associated with courage and gods. These colors will make Holi a festival full of joy and positive energy.

Origin & Meaning

History of Holi

Holi will be one of the most joyful and colorful festivals celebrated in India and many other parts of the world. Known as the "Festival of Colors," it will mark the arrival of spring and will symbolize happiness, love, and the victory of good over evil.

During Holi, people will throw colored powders and water at each other.

Rituals & Culture

Traditions & Preparation

On the eve of the festival of color, a large number of people will assemble to turn on a big fire of joy. It will be a ritual of purification. they will grow some coconut to symbolize the victory of good over darkness.

Cuisine & Flavors

Typical Holi Food

Holi will also be known for its several special food dishes.

🥟

Gujiya

A sweet pastry filled with milk, coconut, and dried fruits. Crispy, golden, and very popular during Holi celebrations.

Most Popular
🥞

Malpua

A soft and sweet pancake made with flour and milk, fried until golden and covered with fragrant sugar syrup.

Traditional
🫓

Puran Poli

A sweet flatbread stuffed with lentils and jaggery. Soft and deeply traditional.

Regional
🥛

Thandai

A cold milk drink blended with nuts, spices, and rose petals. The signature refreshing beverage of the festival.
